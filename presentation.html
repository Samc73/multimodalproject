Topic & Ethical Orientation

"Cognitive enhancement isn't a level playing field. Who has access, who faces pressure, and who bears the risks are all shaped by inequality."

  • Topic: Non-medical use of prescription stimulants (Adderall, Ritalin) among college students
  • Ethical Lens: Equity and access—examining how socioeconomic status determines who can enhance and who gets left behind
  • Key Question: How do existing inequalities shape the landscape of cognitive enhancement?

Challenges Encountered

⚠️ Challenge: Tone & Credibility

How do you discuss drug use with college students without sounding preachy, judgmental, or like a "Just Say No" campaign they'll immediately dismiss?

✓ Solution: Center Student Experience

Used an interactive scenario that starts with the student's perspective. Let them choose what matters to them, then connect to research. No lectures—just reflections.

⚠️ Challenge: Complexity vs. Accessibility

The equity issues are nuanced—healthcare access, socioeconomic factors, systemic pressures. How do you communicate this without overwhelming readers?

✓ Solution: Visual Hierarchy & Chunking

Used statistics as entry points, icon-based equity cards for scanning, and progressive disclosure through the interactive elements.

8 / 10

What I Learned

"The goal isn't to tell people what to think—it's to give them better tools for thinking."

  • Translation is design: Every choice about layout, color, and interaction shapes how the message lands
  • Audience skepticism is valid: Students have good reasons to distrust "health messaging"—earn their attention
  • Equity reframes everything: Shifting from "is this cheating?" to "who has access?" opens new ethical territory
  • Resources matter: Don't just inform—provide pathways to support
